My journey

I studied a Ba (Hons) in Visual Communication at the University of Creative Arts, UK. Here I conjured up a mix of creative passions that included graphic design, printmaking and illustration. I understand that drawing is an important part of the creative process, complimenting traditional skills with a solid understanding of the Adobe Creative Suite, including Photoshop, Illustrator and InDesign.

Having completed several interns including RKCR, London, I freelanced on designs projects that included working with Sony Ericsson, Royal Opera House and Topshop/Topman I love their clothes so much!

Having always wanted to teach, I studied a Post Graduate Certificate in Art and Design Education at the University of Hertfordshire and became a teacher of the visual arts to complement my freelancing. In my first teaching post at Harris Boys Academy, London I taught fine art and graphic communication, as well as starting a qualification in photography.

Using my experiences, I became Head of Creative Media at North Herts College, Hitchin and lead a team of lecturers to deliver a digital media curriculum with qualifications with the University of the Arts, London. Throughout my teaching career, I have also freelanced completing work such as the branding of Cambridge based frozen yogurt franchise Chill, and language learning app Second Accent.

As you can see from this hand-made site, I continue to 'sharpen up my skills', and have recently completed a Front-End Web Development course with Bitmaker, Toronto in which I learnt HTML, CSS and JavaScript to complement my other skills.
